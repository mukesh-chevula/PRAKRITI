AYURVEDA • SEPT 21st '23

Basic Home Remedies you must try out!

In Ayurveda, remedies refer to natural and holistic approaches to healing and maintaining overall health and well-being. Ayurvedic remedies are based on the principles of this ancient Indian system of medicine, which emphasizes balance and harmony in the body, mind, and spirit. Here's a description of some common Ayurvedic remedies and practices:

Herbal Remedies:

Ayurveda makes extensive use of herbs and botanicals for healing. Herbal remedies are prepared from a wide range of plants, roots, leaves, and seeds. They are used to address various health issues, from digestive disorders to skin conditions. Some well-known Ayurvedic herbs include turmeric, ashwagandha, neem, and triphala.

Dietary Remedies:

Ayurveda places significant emphasis on diet as a means of maintaining health and addressing imbalances. Ayurvedic dietary recommendations are personalized based on an individual's constitution (Prakriti) and imbalances (Vikriti). The emphasis is on consuming whole, seasonal, and fresh foods and avoiding processed or incompatible food combinations. Spices are used not only for flavor but also for their medicinal properties.

Yoga and Pranayama

Yoga and Pranayama (breath control) are integral parts of Ayurveda. These practices are used to promote physical fitness, mental clarity, and emotional balance. They can be tailored to an individual's constitution and specific health needs.

Meditation and Mindfulness

Ayurveda recognizes the importance of mental and emotional well-being. Meditation and mindfulness practices are used to reduce stress, improve focus, and cultivate inner peace. These practices vary depending on an individual's dosha and mental state.

Panchakarma

Panchakarma is a comprehensive Ayurvedic detoxification and cleansing therapy. It involves a series of therapeutic treatments to eliminate toxins, restore balance, and rejuvenate the body. Panchakarma is typically performed under the guidance of an Ayurvedic practitioner and includes therapies such as oil massages, steam treatments, and herbal enemas.

Aromatherapy

Essential oils and aromatherapy play a role in Ayurvedic remedies. Aromatherapy can be used for relaxation, stress relief, and to balance the doshas. Different essential oils are recommended for different dosha types.

Lifestyle Adjustments

Ayurveda provides guidelines for daily routines (Dinacharya) that align with the body's natural rhythms. This includes recommendations for waking up early, performing self-care rituals, and going to bed at a reasonable hour. Lifestyle adjustments are personalized based on one's dosha.

Ayurvedic Massage

Abhyanga, or Ayurvedic oil massage, is a common practice to nourish the skin, relax the muscles, and promote overall well-being. Specific oils and techniques are chosen based on an individual's constitution and imbalances.

Gemstone and Metal Remedies

Ayurveda incorporates the use of gemstones and metals for therapeutic purposes, often known as Ratna and Rasa Shastra. These remedies are believed to influence energy and balance in the body.

About Prakriti

Ayurveda is a natural health care system that emphasizes the treatment of disease in a highly individualized manner as it believes that every individual is unique having a different constitution. It classifies all individuals into different 'Prakruti' types based on the theory of tridosha and each type has a varying degree of predisposition to different diseases. This is independent of racial, ethnic, or geographical considerations. Knowing which particular Prakruti one belongs to, enables them to decide the preference of food which suits them best. By following the rules and regulations regarding food and lifestyle, one can prevent themselves from getting inflicted with various diseases. But, Prakruti assessment is a tedious process that requires the individual to visit the physician followed by a long list of questions in order to reach a conclusion. In order to make this process more convenient, the development of a self- assessment tool with the help of artificial intelligence may prove to be a stepping stone. A Chatbot which has pre-recorded questionnaire may help in the Prakruti assessment depending upon the responses given by an individual.
